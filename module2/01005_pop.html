BusinessWeek—Article Title: THE BEST MEDICAL CARE IN THE U.S.

(How Veterans Affairs transformed itself – and what it means for the rest of us). The centerpiece of that culture is VistA, the VA’s much praised electronic medical-records system. Every office visit, prescription, and medical procedure is recorded in its database, allowing doctors and nurses to update themselves on a patient’s status with just a few keystrokes. In 1995, patient records at VA hospitals were available at the time of a clinical encounter only 60% of the time. Today they are 100% available. Some 96% of all prescriptions and medical orders, such as lab tests, are now entered electronically. The national comparison is more like 8%. “One out of five tests in a civilian hospital have to be repeated because the paper results are lost,” says Veterans Affairs Secretary R. James Nicholson. “That's not happening in our hospitals.” VistA is a big reason why the VA has held its costs per patient steady over the past 10 years despite double-digit inflation in health-care prices. http://www.businessweek.com/magazine/content/06_29/b3993061.htm
